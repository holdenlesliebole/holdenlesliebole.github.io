\section{Research Interests}
I am a physical oceanographer, engineer, and geologist working on applied and environmental fluid dynamics, with a dissertation focus on the mechanics of sandy beach change. My PhD investigates how waves drive cross-shore sediment redistribution and post-storm recovery on the San Diego County coastline, using synchronous \emph{in situ} hydrodynamic arrays, repeat topographic and bathymetric surveys, satellite-derived shoreline analysis, and analytical transport models. Alongside the coastal work I keep side projects in adjacent areas of fluid mechanics, including wall-bounded turbulence, surface-layer dynamics, and the general problem of the working distance between governing equations and what field data can resolve. I expect to carry this broader methodological toolkit into future applied and engineering work. My prior experience spans mechanical and civil engineering, climate physics, and international climate policy. I plan to sit for the California civil FE exam in 2026 and the PE exam in 2027.
