I am a physical oceanographer finishing my PhD with a focus on the mechanics of wave-coast interactions, coastal geomorphology, and sediment transport. My work bridges the gap between fundamental fluid physics and real-world engineering problems faced by coastal and hydraulic engineers.

My research combines analytical theory, high-fidelity numerical simulation (including CFD and spectral wave models), field instrumentation, and satellite remote sensing to understand how waves shape coastlines and drive sediment redistribution across scales from individual storm events to long-term geomorphic change. I am seeking roles in civil engineering, mechanical engineering, or environmental consulting where I can apply expertise in fluid mechanics, data analysis, and computational modeling to complex applied problems.
