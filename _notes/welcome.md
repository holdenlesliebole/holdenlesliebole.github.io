This section is my idea for a way to share and make durable many of the notes that I've taken throughout my PhD. As any PhD student will understand, there have been many times where questions that I thought I had settled have ended up resurfacing several months later. This page is a place to write things down in a form I can search and share with friends. I plan to digitize and reorganize many of my old course notes here as well.

## Overview

The ideas here will fit into three rough categories, based on how finished and robust the material is.

1. **Theory notes.** When I've worked something out carefully (e.g. a derivation or an alternative framing of a tool from the literature), I will type it up here. These will be properly LaTeX formatted with figures.

2. **Course notes.** Here I also will type up the lecture notes from some of my most useful graduate courses, often with my own commentary or worked exercises. This is for the purpose of sharing and hopefully helping others make sense of topics that can take a while to learn. Originals will be linked as PDFs where I kept them.

3. **Musings.** I also have more half-formed ideas, speculative arguments, open questions than I know what to do with, and I'll put some of those here too just for fun. These will be marked clearly to caution anyone reading to cite them at their own risk.

If you find errors in any of these documents, or if anything here is useful or resonates, let me know and I'd love to talk further.

## A small MathJax/LaTeX test

I have built MathJax into this section to be able to write things like the small-amplitude dispersion relation

$$
\omega^2 = g k \tanh(k h),
$$

or to note that the shallow-water limit ($k h \ll 1$) reduces this to $c = \sqrt{g h}$, or, inline, remark that $\omega = 2\pi f$ in my convention, without awkward rendering.

If math above renders as LaTeX source instead of typeset equations, something is wrong with the MathJax load and please email me.
